My Journey Into Software

My path into software is a bit unique. After studying physics for a few semesters, I decided to take a break from school and focus on saving up money.

I was working in customer support at a start-up at the time. As I got to know the product and business through answering tickets, I became interested in how SaaS companies are built and operate. I often stayed after hours getting to know the engineers and asking them tons of questions about how things worked.

I was endlessly fascinated by how code on a computer screen could create real-world value if you could just figure out the right problems to solve with it. That pursuit of the right problem was so enticing that I decided to drop out of college and start a career in software.

Members of the engineering team graciously mentored me. Over the course of about 10 months, I learned the basics of software and Ruby on Rails until the fateful day I transferred from the Customer Support team to the Engineering Team.

Now, half a decade later, I realize how valuable it was to work in customer support before entering the world of software.

I think a lot of software engineers like to view their work as isolated from the end user and the business. They tend to be concerned with making sure their code is "correct" as opposed to making sure it is driving the business forward and delighting the end user.

My experience in customer support enables me to view SaaS product development through the lens of user experience and business impact. That is my superpower.

I consider how the code I write will be interacted with by the user or other teams within the company. I like to understand the business implications of my work so I can make sure what I am shipping moves the company forward and is future and growth conscious.

All the time spent troubleshooting technical issues over the phone and tickets honed my ability to adapt how I communicate technical information in a way that is concise and accessible to whoever I am working with. This has been another superpower when working across teams and with non-technical project stakeholders.

I have worked directly with Founders, Product Managers, Operations, Finance, Customer Support, and Engineers to design and ship entirely new software suites as well as optimize existing ones. I pride myself on being able to work with just about anybody to make awesome software.
